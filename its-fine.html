IT'S FINE

Narrative Short Film

Synopsis

Judy, a seventeen year old girl, wakes up after a strange dream like state, sore and naked on her living room floor. She tries to get up before her step-dad stops her. In shock, Judy must figure out how to recover. After a long bath and some time alone, she must endure a family dinner with her step-dad and mother that same night. Quiet and despondent, Judy grapples with her trauma while trying to determine whether to overcome her fear of her step-dad and confide in her mother or whether to suffer in silence.

Director's Statement

RAINN, the nation's largest anti-sexual violence organization claims in a 2018 study that in the United States of America, every 9 minutes, child protective services substantiates, or finds evidence for, a claim of sexual abuse. These statistics are upsetting and shocking, and they only refer to the cases that the government knows about. What about the cases that are never reported/investigated? Why aren't more people talking about this? Several years ago, a friend of mine was brave enough to share their story of familial sexual assault and the impact it has had on their life. After hearing their stories, I was overcome by emotion and deep sadness by the horrors of what they had experienced. What I was stunned most by was the fact that they kept the abuse a secret for so long. I couldn't help but ponder why they wouldn't tell anyone and what that experience must have been like. IT'S FINE is a story of power, what it is like to have your agency ripped away from you and how one young woman copes in the face of these complex issues. With this film I hope to demonstrate the emotional complexities regarding sexual abuse and educate people on real life circumstances that people deal with every day.
